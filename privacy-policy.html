Raw Sensor Multitool — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 12, 2026

Overview

Raw Sensor Multitool is a sensor dashboard app that displays real-time data from your iPhone's built-in sensors. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is used.

Sensor Data

The app reads data from the following sensors: accelerometer, gyroscope, magnetometer, barometric altimeter, and compass. All sensor data is:

  • Displayed on-screen in real time
  • Never stored, logged, or saved to disk
  • Never transmitted to any server or third party
  • Discarded immediately when you leave the app

Location Permission

The app requests "When In Use" location access solely to enable the true north calculation for the compass display. Your location is:

  • Used only to compute magnetic declination (the offset between magnetic north and true north)
  • Never stored or recorded
  • Never transmitted off your device
  • Never used for advertising, tracking, or analytics

Location permission is optional. If denied, the compass will display magnetic heading only.

Advertising

Raw Sensor Multitool displays banner ads provided by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect certain device information to serve ads. For details on how Google uses data in their advertising products, please review Google's Privacy Policy.

We do not sell or share any personal data with advertisers beyond what AdMob collects through its SDK.

Analytics and Tracking

Other than the AdMob SDK, Raw Sensor Multitool contains no analytics tools, crash reporters, or tracking frameworks. We do not collect usage statistics or any form of telemetry.

Data Collection Summary

We collect no personal data. All sensor readings are processed on-device and are never stored or transmitted. The only third-party data collection is through the AdMob advertising SDK.

Children's Privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, we will post the revised version here with an updated date.
